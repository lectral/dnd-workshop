# NPC Designer Reference

## Table-first NPC checklist

### Identity
- Name
- Role in the story
- Immediate reason to exist in the next scene

### Play signals
- First visual impression
- One physical tell
- One speech pattern or verbal habit
- Emotional baseline under no pressure

### Dramatic engine
- Goal
- Fear
- Secret
- Leverage over the party or world
- What they offer if engaged

### Practical DM use
- Best scene type: negotiation, ambush, clue delivery, patron briefing, betrayal, comic relief, moral pressure
- What they do if ignored
- What they do if threatened
- What they do if respected

### Optional combat layer
If the NPC can fight, add only what the scene needs:
- CR or threat band
- Signature action loop
- One defensive trick
- One social or exploration edge
- Quick reskin notes

## Handbook-style add-ons for major NPCs

- `Meta`: scent, clothing, status signals, entourage, lair or workplace
- `Tactics`: opening move, preferred range, retreat threshold, ally coordination
- `Roleplaying`: sample phrases, bargaining angle, agitation tells
- `Lore`: what the party can know at different DCs
- `Rescaling`: bodyguards, elite upgrade, magic backup, escape plan
